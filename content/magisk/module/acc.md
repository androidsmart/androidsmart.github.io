ACC works by controlling the charging switch, which is a kernel parameter that determines whether the device is charging or not. By toggling the charging switch on and off, ACC can regulate the charging process according to your preferences. For example, you can set a maximum charge level, such as 80%, and ACC will stop charging when the battery reaches that level. You can also set a minimum charge level, such as 40%, and ACC will resume charging when the battery drops below that level. This way, you can keep your battery within a safe and optimal range.

ACC can also limit the charging current, which is the amount of electric current that flows into the battery. By reducing the charging current, you can lower the temperature and stress of the battery, which can prolong its lifespan. You can specify the maximum charging current in milliamps, such as 1000 mA, and ACC will adjust the charging switch accordingly.

ACC can also limit the charging voltage, which is the electric potential difference between the battery terminals. By lowering the charging voltage, you can reduce the risk of overcharging and overheating the battery, which can damage its cells. You can specify the maximum charging voltage in millivolts, such as 4100 mV, and ACC will monitor the battery voltage and control the charging switch.

## How to install and use ACC

To install ACC, you need a rooted Android device with Magisk installed. You can download the latest version of ACC from GitHub or Magisk Manager. You can also watch this video for a detailed tutorial on how to install ACC.

To use ACC, you can either use the terminal commands or the plugins. The terminal commands allow you to configure and control ACC from the command line. You can access the terminal from any terminal emulator app, such as Termux, or from the Magisk Manager app. To see the list of available commands, type `acc -h` in the terminal. You can also type `acc -i` to see the current status and configuration of ACC.

The plugins are scripts that run automatically when certain events occur, such as boot, shutdown, or battery level change. You can use the plugins to automate the behavior of ACC, such as switching between different profiles or modes. You can find the plugins in the `/data/adb/vr25/acc-data/plugins` directory. You can also create your own plugins using the template provided by ACC.

## What benefits ACC can offer

ACC can offer many benefits for your Android device, such as:

- Extending the battery service life by preventing overcharging, overheating, and excessive current.
- Saving battery power by stopping charging when the battery is full or near full, and resuming charging when the battery is low or near low.
- Improving the battery health by keeping the battery within a safe and optimal range of capacity, temperature, and voltage.
- Enhancing the device performance by reducing the thermal throttling and battery drain caused by fast charging.
- Customizing the charging experience by choosing from various profiles and modes, or creating your own.

ACC is a powerful and versatile tool for managing the charging process of your Android device. It can help you get the most out of your battery and device, while also protecting them from harm. If you are looking for a way to improve your battery life and health, you should give ACC a try.
